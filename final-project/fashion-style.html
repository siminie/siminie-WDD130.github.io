Chic Style

Chic, meaning "stylish" or "smart", is an element of fashion. It was originally a French word. The Chic Style Type is known for wearing monochromatic ensembles and pieces with sleek clean lines. In the Chic Style wardrobe, you'll find sleek, modern shapes and of-the-moment accessories. A little black dress is always a staple, as is a little white dress.

Formal Style

Formal wear or full dress is the traditional Western dress code category applicable for most formal occasions, such as weddings, christenings, confirmations, funerals, Easter, and Christmas traditions. In addition to certain state dinners, audiences, balls, and horse racing events. Formal wear includes a tuxedo or formal dark suit and matching tie for men and appropriate dresses, skirts, shirts, trousers for women.

Preppy Style

Preppy style is a subculture in the United States associated with old private Northeastern college preparatory schools. The term preppy derives from the private, university-preparatory, or prep schools that some American upper class and upper-middle-class children attend. Examples of preppy attire include argyle sweaters, chinos, button-down Oxford cloth shirts, pearl necklaces and earrings, gold bangle or large chain bracelets, penny loafers, polo shirts.

Streetwear Style

Streetwear is a style of casual clothing that became global in the 1990s. It grew from New York hip hop fashion and eventually California surf skate culture, to encompass elements of sportswear, punk, and Japanese street fashion. Eventually, Haute fashion design became an influence. Streetwear brands include Nike, Supreme, Adidas Yeezy, Champion, and more.

Casual Style

Casual wear is a Western dress code that is relaxed, occasional, spontaneous, and suited for everyday use. Casual wear became popular in the Western world following the counterculture of the 1960s. When emphasizing casual wear's comfort, it may be referred to as leisurewear. This includes jeans, sweaters, t-shirts, sneakers, and much more.
